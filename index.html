City in Nature, Nature to Play

City in Nature is the blueprint for Singapore's future, with parks playing a crucial role. According to the Singapore Green Plan 2030, by 2030, all households will be within a 10-minute walk of a park. Additionally, by 2035, the city aims to add 1,000 hectares of green spaces, further enhancing its commitment to sustainability and urban livability.

The storymap illustrates the distribution of existing park resources in Singapore, per capita park area, and the accessibility of current parks. Through this map, we can identify from a regional planning perspective which areas require more parks and how much additional park space is needed to meet the goals of the Green Plan.

We aim not only to meet the quantitative demand for parks from residents but also to improve the quality of parks so they can truly attract residents to play.

The crowdsourcing map is designed to gather information on the parks that residents love, allowing us to understand why these parks are appealing. From a place-making perspective, this helps us enhance the quality of parks.
